About Me

As a Research Scientist at A*STAR's Institute for Human Development and Potential (IHDP), I am dedicated to advancing mental health research, with a focus on cognition, mood, and stress.

My work is centered on understanding and developing effective, accessible stress-reduction strategies. A cornerstone of my career has been leading a large-scale, multi-site project on self-administered mindfulness. This international collaboration involved 61 researchers across 37 sites and 2,239 participants. Our findings, published in Nature Human Behaviour, demonstrated that mindfulness significantly reduces stress compared to a control.

The impact of this study has led to significant opportunities, including being invited as a Keynote Speaker at the 4th Annual International Conference of Thai Clinical Psychologists and an invitation to present at the prestigious Global Young Scientists Summit (GYSS) 2025.

This project was built upon my foundational doctoral research at Université Grenoble Alpes and Swansea University, where I conducted two comprehensive meta-analyses. The first, a Registered Report, evaluated the efficacy of nature exposure and social support. The second synthesized evidence for self-administered mindfulness and biofeedback, identifying critical gaps in the literature that my multi-site study aimed to address.

Since joining A*STAR, I have expanded my expertise into clinical trials and digital biomarkers, leading a fully decentralized pilot trial of a mindfulness intervention for Singaporeans that used Heart Rate Variability (HRV) to measure stress.

I earned my Ph.D. in Cognitive Sciences, Psychology, and Neurocognition with distinction for excellence in work and exemplary Open Science practices. My academic background also includes a Master's in Social and Occupational Psychology from the University of Milano-Bicocca. Through these experiences, I have honed my ability to lead impactful, international research and collaborate with interdisciplinary teams to enhance mental well-being.

Current Project

Building Resilience Across Socioeconomic Contexts: A Multi-Site study on Mindfulness

As Principal Investigator, I am leading this multi-site study funded by the National Medical Research Council (NMRC) of Singapore. This project aims to discover the optimal way to structure mindfulness training for stress reduction by testing different ratios of formal vs. informal practice. A key goal is to ensure health equity by recruiting a diverse, international sample.

We are still looking for international collaborators and community partners. You can read more about the project on its Open Science Framework page or contact me directly for more information.
